## 1. Executive Summary & Vision

### 1.1 Core Mission
**SmartCare ICU** is a state-of-the-art, paperless intensive care management and AI decision-support platform designed to digitize the entire clinical lifecycle—from initial patient admission to treatment authorization and discharge. By replacing paper charts with a unified, high-speed clinical dashboard, SmartCare ICU eliminates manual charting delays, reduces medical transcription errors, and combats cognitive overload during high-pressure shifts.

### 1.2 Strategic Value Proposition
Intensive Care Units operate under extreme time pressure where fragmented data can cost lives. SmartCare ICU bridges the gap between raw, continuous physiological telemetry and rapid clinical decision-making. Through **conversational Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)**, **instant 24-hour AI summarization**, and an **Autonomous Monitoring Agent** armed with **Clinical Reasoning Explanations**, the platform empowers nursing staff, medical residents, and attending specialists with real-time, actionable medical intelligence.

## 2. Problem Statement & Clinical Objectives

### 2.1 The ICU Bottleneck
1. **Paper & Charting Friction:** ICU nurses spend countless hours hand-writing hourly vital signs, fluid balances, and Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) scores, detaching them from direct bedside care.
2. **Cognitive Overload During Rounds:** When senior specialists arrive for morning clinical rounds, they must manually sift through dozens of pages of progress notes, lab PDFs, and shift logs to form a cohesive picture of the patient's trajectory over the last 24 hours.
3. **Delayed Alert Escalation:** Subtle trends (e.g., gradual widening of pulse pressure accompanied by dropping urine output) are often missed until critical decompensation occurs because static charts do not proactively synthesize multi-variable anomalies.

### 2.2 Clinical & Operational Objectives
- **Zero-Friction Paperless Workflow:** 100% digital data capture upon patient admission, vitals logging, and lab/radiology indexing.
- **Instantaneous Context Retrieval:** Reduce the time required to answer specific historical clinical queries across shifts from minutes to `<3 seconds` via conversational RAG.
- **Cognitive Reduction via AI Summarization:** Provide a 1-click, reasoned overview of a patient’s 24-hour clinical evolution in `<5 seconds`.
- **Proactive Vigilance:** Automatically detect abnormal physiological trends and push alerts accompanied by transparent clinical reasoning before emergency thresholds are crossed.

## 3. User Personas & Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) Architecture

### 3.1 User Roles & Behavioral Profiles

#### 1. The Data Creators (`Role: "Nurse"`)
- **Who they are:** Charge nurses and shift nurses operating directly at the bedside under intense time constraints.
- **Core Workflow:** Create initial digital patient profiles upon ICU admission; log hourly vital signs (HR, BP, SpO2, Temp, MAP), fluid intake/output (I/O), and Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) scores; instantly upload incoming lab results and radiology PDFs.
- **System Permissions:** **Create / Write / Edit** access for clinical vitals, profiles, and document uploads. No access to administrative settings; streamlined view of AI summarization tools to prioritize rapid, accurate data entry.

#### 2. The Data Consumers & Executors (`Role: "Resident"`)
- **Who they are:** Medical residents managing minute-by-minute patient care across long shifts.
- **Core Workflow:** Perform initial clinical assessments; log comprehensive medical histories; query the RAG system for instant historical metrics across shift boundaries (e.g., *"What was the average blood sugar and insulin titration during the night shift?"*); review and triage alerts generated by the Autonomous Monitoring Agent.
- **System Permissions:** **Full Read / Write** clinical access; full access to conversational RAG querying, AI summarization, and diagnostic reasoning explanations.

#### 3. The Decision Makers (`Role: "Specialist"`)
- **Who they are:** Senior ICU consultants and attending physicians carrying ultimate clinical responsibility.
- **Core Workflow:** Open the Unified Patient Dashboard during clinical rounds; trigger one-click **Instant AI Summarization** for rapid, reasoned overviews; evaluate AI-generated differential diagnoses and clinical reasoning explanations; officially authorize treatment modifications and finalize hospital discharges.
- **System Permissions:** **Full Read / Write / Approve** clinical access. Exclusive permission to execute official treatment approvals and discharge actions within the system.

#### 4. The Facilitators (`Role: "System Admin"`)
- **Who they are:** Hospital IT infrastructure staff and departmental administrators.
- **Core Workflow:** Provision and revoke accounts for medical staff; assign and audit role boundaries; monitor system health, database backups, and LLM API rate/token utilization.
- **System Permissions:** **System Admin** access to infrastructure configuration and account management. **Strictly No Access** to unblinded clinical records, vitals, or patient identifiers to enforce strict medical privacy and HIPAA compliance.

## 4. Authentication, Authorization & Legal Compliance

### 4.1 Authentication Lifecycle (Identity Verification)
1. **Login Request:** The user submits their professional credentials (email and password) via the React (`client/`) frontend over HTTPS.
2. **Backend Verification:** The Express/Node.js (`server/`) backend validates credentials against hashed user records (`bcrypt`) stored in **PostgreSQL**.
3. **Token Generation:** Upon verification, the server signs a **JSON Web Token (JWT)** containing the user's `id` and strict `role` (e.g., `{ id: "64a7...", role: "Nurse" }`).
4. **Secure Cookie Delivery:** To prevent Cross-Site Scripting (XSS) attacks in a healthcare environment, the JWT is delivered inside an `HttpOnly`, `Secure`, `SameSite=Strict` cookie (`localStorage` storage is strictly prohibited).
5. **Authenticated Requests:** All subsequent API requests automatically transmit the `HttpOnly` cookie. Backend middleware validates token authenticity and expiration before processing any route logic.

### 4.2 Authorization Middleware (RBAC Enforcement)
- Every Express endpoint under `/api/clinical/*` and `/api/admin/*` must pass through authentication and role-checking middleware (`verifyToken` and `requireRole([...allowedRoles])`).
- Any attempt by an unauthorized role (e.g., an ICU Nurse attempting to trigger a final discharge approval, or a System Admin attempting to query patient clinical vitals) must immediately abort with `HTTP 403 Forbidden` and trigger an alert in the action audit log.

### 4.3 Legal Compliance & Data Preservation
1. **Soft Deletion (Archive State):** Medical records can never be permanently wiped from the database. Every PostgreSQL table (`patients`, `vital_signs`, `clinical_notes`, `lab_reports`) must include `is_archived BOOLEAN DEFAULT FALSE` and `archived_at TIMESTAMP WITH TIME ZONE DEFAULT NULL`. Deletion requests simply toggle this flag (`UPDATE ... SET is_archived = true, archived_at = NOW()`), hiding records from standard dashboard views while preserving full relational data integrity for legal audits.
2. **Action Audit Logging Middleware:** A dedicated Express middleware must intercept and record every data creation, modification, archival, and AI query action. Each audit log entry must immutably capture:
   - `timestamp`: UTC exact time.
   - `userId`: ID and role of the acting user.
   - `action`: Exact CRUD operation (`CREATE_PATIENT`, `LOG_VITALS`, `QUERY_RAG`, `APPROVE_TREATMENT`, etc.).
   - `targetEntityId`: ID of the affected patient or record.
   - `ipAddress`: Client IP address for forensic tracking.

## 5. Strategic Design & UX Specifications (from `PRODUCT.md`)

### 5.1 Register & Platform Ergonomics
- **Register (`product`):** Design strictly serves clinical clarity, speed, and precision. Every visual element must enhance situational awareness and eliminate cognitive friction during emergency situations.
- **Platform (`adaptive`):** The interface must render flawlessly on both desktop hospital terminals (`1080p` to `4K` widescreen) and bedside iPad/tablet carts (`1024px` width touch screens).
  - Touch targets for vital signs entry buttons and AI triggers must maintain a minimum hit area of `48x48px`.
  - Layout grids must utilize fluid responsive spacing (`clamp()` and `repeat(auto-fit, minmax(280px, 1fr))`) without horizontal overflow or clipped popovers.

### 5.2 Strategic Design Principles
1. **Uncluttered Vigilance Above All:** Every pixel must serve clinical speed. Information hierarchy naturally directs the eye to critical physiological shifts, visual trend sparklines, and abnormal AI telemetry alerts without decorative noise.
2. **High-Precision Ergonomics:** Forms must support both rapid touch input (large numerical keypads for bedside tablets) and high-speed keyboard tabbing (`Enter` and `Tab` progression) with smart boundary validation.
3. **Zero Ambiguity in Clinical Alerts:** AI monitoring alerts (`P0 Emergency`, `P1 Abnormal`) must command immediate visual priority using distinct iconography, typography weights, and clear border framing. Never hide critical data inside hover-only states (`<img>` hover animations are strictly banned).
4. **Strict Role-Driven Boundaries:** The UI dynamically adapts to the user's JWT role—hiding complex RAG chat panels from shift nurses during rapid vitals entry, while surfacing prominent summarization and approval action bars for attending specialists during rounds.
5. **Legible at 4:00 AM (`≥4.5:1` Strict Contrast):** Whether viewed under bright hospital fluorescent lights or in a dimmed night-shift room, contrast across body text, numerical vital metrics, and status badges must rigorously adhere to WCAG AA (`≥4.5:1` for body text, `≥3:1` for large bold metrics ≥18px).

### 5.3 Anti-References & Hard Design Bans
- **No Low-Contrast Text:** Strictly banned. Light gray body text on white (`<4.5:1` contrast) or washed-out gray text on colored status badges is a critical clinical defect.
- **No Decorative Glassmorphism or Blurs:** Frosted glass (`backdrop-blur`) and background gradients degrade readability and introduce GPU rendering latency on hospital cart computers.
- **No Card Grid Slop or Nested Cards:** Avoid repeating identical card layouts and boxed-within-boxes container borders (`border-left` side-stripe accents). Use clean data tables and grouped visual sections.
- **No Decorative Text Gradients or Eyebrow Kicker Spam:** Never use `background-clip: text` gradients on headings or tiny all-caps tracked `01 / 02 / 03` eyebrows above every section.
- **No Hover-Gated Critical Information:** All diagnostic reasoning, alert timestamps, and action buttons must be accessible without requiring mouse hover over hidden triggers.

## 6. Functional Requirements (MVP Scope)

### Phase 1: Foundation & Data Integrity (Weeks 1–2)

#### FR-1.1: Secure Authentication & Role-Based Authorization Engine
- **Description:** Implement the end-to-end authentication flow using `HttpOnly` JWT cookies and Express role checks.
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Login endpoint validates credentials and issues an `HttpOnly`, `Secure` JWT cookie expiring in 12 hours.
  - Express RBAC middleware rejects unauthorized role requests with `HTTP 403` and logs the attempt.
  - Frontend **Zustand store** (`useAuthStore`) decodes and manages session metadata upon app initialization, conditionally rendering navigation and action items based on `user.role` with reactive state synchronization.

#### FR-1.2: Smart Input Validation & Real-Time Physiological Boundary Checks
- **Description:** Prevent erroneous medical data entry both on the React frontend and Express backend before database persistence.
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Frontend numerical inputs enforce clinical biological limits (e.g., Heart Rate `20–300 bpm`, MAP `20–200 mmHg`, SpO2 `50–100%`, Temperature `30.0–45.0°C`, GCS `3–15`).
  - Out-of-range inputs highlight instantly with clear descriptive error messages (`aria-invalid="true"` and `aria-describedby`) without blocking emergency override entries if flagged by the clinician as confirmed critical values.
  - Backend schema validation (`Zod` or `Joi` in Express middleware) double-checks boundaries before PostgreSQL transaction commit.

#### FR-1.3: Soft Deletion (Archive State) & Action Audit Logging
- **Description:** Implement data archiving and comprehensive audit logging for medical audit readiness.
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Deleting a patient profile, vital entry, or note toggles `isArchived: true` and records `archivedAt`. Queries filter `isArchived: false` by default.
  - Audit logging middleware intercepts all write/archive endpoints and inserts an immutable record into the `audit_logs` table in PostgreSQL within `<10ms` transaction overhead.

#### FR-1.4: Paperless ICU Workflow CRUD Endpoints
- **Description:** Core REST APIs enabling complete digital patient management without paper charts.
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - `POST /api/patients`: Create patient admission profile (Name, Age, MRN, Blood Type, Allergies, Code Status, Attending Specialist).
  - `POST /api/vitals`: Log hourly physiological vitals and GCS scores with timestamp and nurse ID.
  - `POST /api/vitals/fluid`: Log fluid intake (IV infusions, enteral feeds) and output (urine, drains) in milliliters (`mL`).
  - `POST /api/documents`: Upload lab result PDFs or radiology images (`multer` file indexing with patient linkage).

### Phase 2: Core UX & Clinical Visualization (Weeks 3–4)

#### FR-2.1: Unified Patient Dashboard
- **Description:** Consolidated, high-performance interface providing rapid, friction-free access to all patient files, vital history, and active notes.
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Single-page dashboard layout split into clear, scannable zones: Sticky Context Header, Live Physiological Vitals Panel, Fluid Balance Tracker, Lab & PDF Repository, and Clinical History Notes.
  - Optimized rendering (`React.memo` and virtualized lists where appropriate) ensuring `<500ms` total UI transition time between patient charts.

#### FR-2.2: Sticky Clinical Context Bar
- **Description:** A slim, fixed-position header (`position: sticky; top: 0; z-index: 50;`) displaying absolute critical patient identity metrics that never scroll out of view.
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Must display: Patient Name, Medical Record Number (MRN), Age/Gender, Blood Type (`A+`, `O-`, etc.), Critical Allergies (in high-contrast warning pill), Code Status (`Full Code`, `DNR/DNI`), and Primary Attending Specialist.
  - Stays anchored at the top of the viewport across all tabs and scrolling views.
  - Includes a quick-switcher dropdown allowing clinicians to rapidly search and jump to another active ICU bed/patient without navigating back to the main census table.

#### FR-2.3: Visual Trend Sparklines
- **Description:** Lightweight, inline visual trend graphs embedded directly next to numerical vital metrics to instantly show trajectory over the last 12–24 hours.
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Render crisp SVG sparkline charts inside the vitals table rows for Heart Rate, Mean Arterial Pressure (MAP), SpO2, and GCS.
  - Sparklines must indicate directionality (rising, falling, stable) and highlight abnormal data points with distinct data markers (`circle` node with high-contrast fill and icon tooltip).
  - Must load instantly without blocking UI interactivity.

### Phase 3: Intelligence & AI Automation (Weeks 5–6)

#### FR-3.1: Conversational RAG Interface (n8n & LLM Integration)
- **Description:** Embedded clinical chat interface allowing residents and specialists to query the longitudinal patient database using natural language.
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Connects via webhook to an **n8n orchestration workflow** that vectorizes or structured-queries the patient’s historical vitals, nursing logs, and lab reports.
  - Supports queries such as: *"Show me the trend of potassium levels compared to urine output over the last 3 shifts."* or *"What was the exact GCS score when the patient was first admitted from the ER?"*
  - Responses must include exact timestamps and source citations (`[Log 04:15 AM - Nurse Smith]`).

#### FR-3.2: Instant AI Summarization
- **Description:** One-click summarization button on the Unified Patient Dashboard that synthesizes extensive 24-hour records into rapid, reasoned clinical overviews.
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Clicking **"Instant 24h Summary"** triggers the AI engine to aggregate all vital trends, fluid balances, lab changes, and nursing entries from the past 24 hours.
  - Outputs a structured medical summary categorized by system: **Hemodynamic Status**, **Respiratory & Ventilator State**, **Renal/Fluid Balance**, and **Neurological Status**.
  - Must complete processing and display within `<5 seconds`.

#### FR-3.3: Autonomous Monitoring Agent & Alerting
- **Description:** Proactive background monitoring engine that continuously evaluates incoming vital signs against multi-variable anomaly thresholds and pushes real-time alerts.
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Actively scans incoming telemetry streams for patterns indicating clinical deterioration (e.g., Sepsis trajectory: dropping MAP + rising HR + spiking temp; or Respiratory failure: dropping SpO2 + rising respiratory rate).
  - Pushes real-time notifications to the dashboard top alert banner with clear severity levels (`P0 Emergency: Immediate Bedside Attention`, `P1 Clinical Warning`).

#### FR-3.4: Clinical Reasoning Explanations
- **Description:** Every AI-generated monitoring alert and diagnostic suggestion must explicitly articulate its underlying medical rationale.
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Alongside every alert or summary conclusion, the interface displays a dedicated **"Clinical Reasoning & Differential"** drawer or expandable panel.
  - Must state: (1) The exact physiological metrics that triggered the conclusion (`MAP dropped from 78 to 58 mmHg while Urine Output decreased to <15 mL/hr over 2 hours`); (2) The primary differential hypotheses (`Consider early septic shock or hypovolemic deficit`); (3) Recommended diagnostic verification steps (`Suggest checking arterial blood gas (ABG) and serum lactate`).

## 7. Non-Functional & Technical Architecture Requirements

### 7.1 Technology Stack & System Boundaries
- **Frontend Core:** React 19 (`client/src/`) using Vite as the build engine.
- **State Management:** **Zustand** (`client/src/store/`) for lightweight, highly reactive global state management (`useAuthStore`, `usePatientStore`, `useAlertStore`, and `useVitalsStore`), providing zero-boilerplate reactivity and instant slice updates across clinical components without unnecessary re-renders.
- **Styling & Tokens:** Vanilla CSS / Tailwind CSS v4 (`index.css`) built upon OKLCH color variables following `PRODUCT.md` clinical precision standards.
- **Backend Core:** Express / Node.js (`server/index.js`, `server/app.js`) running modular routing and middleware (`routes/`, `controllers/`, `middlewares/`).
- **Database Layer:** **PostgreSQL** relational database (`server/models/`, `server/config/`), managed with connection pooling (`pg` / **Prisma ORM** / **Drizzle ORM**) to guarantee strict ACID compliance, relational integrity across vitals and patient records, and robust indexed queries (`SELECT ... WHERE is_archived = false`).
- **AI Orchestration Layer:** n8n scheduled workflows and webhook endpoints interfacing with foundation large language models (Gemini Pro / GPT-4o) and relational SQL / vector database retrievers.

### 7.2 Performance & Latency Benchmarks
- **Smart Input Validation:** Frontend error feedback `<50ms` after input blur.
- **Dashboard Initial Render:** First contentful paint (FCP) `<500ms` on standard hospital workstations and tablet carts over WiFi.
- **CRUD Endpoint Latency:** API response time `<150ms` (`95th percentile`) for vital sign insertions and patient queries.
- **Conversational RAG Latency:** End-to-end query processing and response streaming within `<3 seconds`.
- **Instant AI Summarization:** Complete 24-hour multi-record synthesis within `<5 seconds`.

### 7.3 Reliability & Security Constraints
- **Uptime Target:** `99.9%` operational availability during critical clinical shift hours.
- **Security & Privacy:** All HTTP traffic encrypted via TLS/HTTPS. Zero exposure of API keys, JWT secrets, or unblinded patient data in client-side bundles or `localStorage`.
- **Graceful Error Recovery:** If the n8n AI webhook or LLM API experiences downtime or timeouts (`>6 seconds`), the frontend must gracefully fall back to displaying raw historical data and clear non-disruptive status banners (`AI Decision Support Temporarily Offline — Standard Clinical Data Fully Available`).

## 8. Future Roadmap Specifications (Post-MVP Version 2.0)

The following advanced operational features are deliberately excluded from the Phase 1–3 MVP to ensure rapid delivery and rigorous testing, but are architecturally planned for Version 2.0:

1. **Multi-Agent Diagnostic Council:** Advanced multi-agent orchestration where specialized AI agents (`Hemodynamic Agent`, `Infectious Disease Agent`, `Neurological Agent`) independently evaluate patient data and debate before presenting a cross-checked consensus recommendation to the specialist.
2. **Automated Shift Handover (End-of-Shift Summary):** Automated generation of a structured, formatted handover report (`SBAR` format: Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation) triggered automatically at 07:00 AM and 07:00 PM shift changes.
3. **Voice-to-Text Clinical Notes:** Bedside speech-to-text dictation utilizing medical-tuned transcription models (`Whisper` / medical speech APIs) allowing doctors and nurses to dictate observations directly into the notes field while keeping their hands sterile.
4. **Morning Round Digest (6:00 AM Automated Briefs):** An n8n scheduled cron workflow (`0 6 * * *`) that compiles 24-hour summaries across all ICU beds and emails a formatted morning rounds digest directly to attending specialists before they arrive at the hospital.
5. **One-Click Discharge Draft:** Queries the RAG system to compile admission complaints, daily progression notes, key surgical/procedural events, and final vital trends into a ready-to-print discharge summary text block.
6. **Graceful Degradation (Offline State Cache):** Service Worker and IndexedDB caching engine that stores the last retrieved state of the patient dashboard, allowing nurses to view vital history and input emergency offline notes even if the hospital network connection temporarily drops.
7. **Automated Long-Term Data Management:** Background database compaction, partitioning of historical vitals older than 30 days into cold storage, and automated ICD-10 medical coding extraction from clinical notes.

## 9. Verification & Testing Plan

### 9.1 Automated Integration & API Testing
- **Auth & RBAC Matrix Verification:** Automated API test suite (`Jest`/`Supertest`) executing requests against endpoints across all 4 roles to verify `HTTP 200/201` for allowed actions and `HTTP 403` for forbidden actions.
- **Smart Validation Boundaries:** Unit tests verifying that edge-case inputs (e.g., Heart Rate `= 19`, `= 301`, `NaN`, `-5`) are rejected with descriptive error codes.
- **Soft Deletion & Audit Trail Integrity:** Integration tests asserting that `DELETE /api/patients/:id` executes an `UPDATE patients SET is_archived = true` transaction and inserts an exact matching immutable row into the `audit_logs` PostgreSQL table without deleting database rows.

### 9.2 Frontend Accessibility (`a11y`) & Ergonomics Verification
- **Contrast Ratio Auditing:** Run automated accessibility scans (`axe-core`) across every dashboard state, ensuring `≥4.5:1` contrast for all body text and `≥3:1` for vital metrics under both dark/slate and light themes.
- **Keyboard & Screen Reader Navigation:** Verify that every modal, patient switcher, and form input can be navigated using only `Tab`, `Shift+Tab`, `Enter`, and `Space`, with visible focus rings (`focus-visible:ring-2`).
- **Reduced Motion Verification:** Verify that enabling `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)` disables sparkline entrance animations and alert pulsing, rendering crisp static visual indicators immediately.

### 9.3 End-to-End Clinical Scenario Validation
- **Scenario A (Rapid Bedside Admission & Vitals Entry by Nurse):** Simulate an ICU Nurse logging in on an iPad touch cart, creating a new patient profile, entering initial 1-hour vitals with GCS calculation, and uploading a PDF blood panel within `<90 seconds` total elapsed time.
- **Scenario B (Night Shift RAG Query & Alert Triage by Resident):** Simulate a Medical Resident receiving a `P1 Clinical Warning` alert from the Autonomous Monitoring Agent, opening the Clinical Reasoning drawer to review the physiological explanation, and querying the RAG system for the patient's 12-hour fluid output trend.
- **Scenario C (Morning Clinical Rounds & Approval by Specialist):** Simulate an ICU Specialist opening the patient dashboard during 08:00 AM rounds, clicking **"Instant 24h Summary"**, reviewing the AI-synthesized hemodynamic conclusions, and executing a final treatment approval action.
